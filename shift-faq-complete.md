## Individual users — /shift page (bottom section)

### How it works

**Does the app really track my trips automatically?**
Yes. For walks, bike rides, buses, and subway trips, Shift detects and logs your
trip in the background using your phone's location and motion sensors. You don't
need to open the app, tap anything, or remember to log anything. Your streak keeps
going and your points keep accumulating whether you open the app or not.

We're still refining transit detection accuracy — if the app can't confidently
match a trip to a route, it may occasionally ask for a single tap to confirm.
That's the exception, not the rule.

**Does it drain my battery?**
Shift uses the same background location technology as navigation apps, optimized
for low power consumption. In typical use, the impact on battery life is minimal —
similar to having Apple Maps or Google Maps running in the background occasionally.

**What counts as an active trip?**
Walking, biking, taking the bus, taking the subway or commuter rail, carpooling,
and e-scooter rides all count. Car trips (driving solo) do not earn points, but
they also don't hurt your standing — they're just not active trips.

**What is my Shift Rate?**
Your Shift Rate is the percentage of your total trips that are active (non-car),
calculated over the last 7 days. It's the core metric in Shift — a simple number
that reflects how much of your getting-around is happening without a car.
Your 30-day and all-time Shift Rate are also visible on your profile.

**What if auto-detection misses a trip?**
You can add a trip manually within 3 days. Manual trips are saved and count toward
your totals, though auto-detected trips carry higher confidence in our verification
system — which matters if you participate in competitive challenges.

### Streaks

**How does the streak work?**
Your streak counts consecutive active days — any day where you complete at least
one active trip. A 2-day grace period applies before your streak breaks: if you go
one or two days without an active trip, your streak stays alive. It resets only
after three consecutive days with no active trips at all.

**Does driving break my streak?**
No. A day where you drive to work but walk to lunch is still an active day.
A day where you only drive simply doesn't count as an active day — but it doesn't
reset anything on its own.

**Will I get a warning before my streak breaks?**
Yes. On the second day without an active trip, Shift sends a single notification:
"Your X-day streak is still going — take an active trip today to keep it alive."
No notification fires when the streak actually resets — it just resets quietly,
and your best-ever streak is always preserved.

**What happens if there's a major MBTA disruption?**
If a significant service disruption affects your typical transit route on a day
you haven't logged an active trip, Shift automatically protects your streak and
notifies you. This protection is available up to 3 times per rolling 30 days.

### Tiers and status

**How do I level up?**
Tiers require two things simultaneously: a cumulative active trip count and a
minimum trailing 60-day Shift Rate.

| Tier | Active trips | Trailing 60-day Shift Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | 0 | None |
| Mover | 25 | 10% |
| Shifter | 100 | 20% |
| Leader | 250 | 30% |
| Trailblazer | 500 | 50% |

**Can I lose my tier?**
Yes, if your trailing 60-day Shift Rate drops below your tier's threshold.
You'll receive a warning at day 30, and if the rate hasn't recovered by day 60,
your tier drops. Your cumulative trip count is never lost — bring your Shift Rate
back above the threshold and your tier comes back with it.

**Why does my tier matter beyond status?**
Higher tiers multiply your points earnings per active trip — from 1 point per trip
at Starter to 5 points per trip at Trailblazer. Staying active at a higher tier
compounds meaningfully over time.

### Points and rewards

**How do I earn points?**
Every active trip earns points, multiplied by your tier. You also earn flat bonus
points for completing your first 30-day challenge, participating in flagship events
like Shift Your Summer, reaching a new tier, maintaining streaks, trying a new mode
for the first time, and referring friends who join and take their first trip.
Car trips earn zero points.

**What can I redeem points for?**
Points are redeemable for discounts and offers from local businesses — things like
Bluebike credits and offers from local shops and services. The catalog grows as we
add partners. Points have no monetary value and cannot be transferred, sold,
or converted to cash.

**Do my points expire?**
Points expire after 12 months of account inactivity. As long as you're taking
active trips, your points are safe.

### Privacy and data

**What data does Shift collect?**
Shift collects location and motion data to detect and log active trips. This data
is used to calculate your Shift Rate, award points, and power the neighborhood
leaderboard. It is not sold to third parties.

**Can my employer see my trips?**
No. Employer partners receive aggregate participation data only — how many employees
are active commuters, total active trips, mode share across the team. They cannot
see any individual user's trips, routes, or Shift Rate.

**Can other users see where I live or work?**
No. The leaderboard shows your display name, tier, and trip count — nothing about
your specific routes or locations.

### Getting started

**Is Shift free?**
Yes. The app is free to download and use. Points and rewards are earned through
active trips — there's nothing to buy.

**When does Shift launch?**
Shift is currently in early access in Cambridge and Somerville. Join the waitlist
to be among the first in your neighborhood.

**What phone do I need?**
Shift is available for iOS and Android. Auto-detection works best with location

**I referred a friend — when do I get my points?**
You receive 25 points when your friend completes onboarding and logs their first
active trip. Your friend also receives 10 points to get them started.

## Employers — /shift/employers page

**Can my company use Shift for a workplace challenge?**
Yes. Shift supports private employer challenges — an invite-code-gated group where
your employees compete on a shared leaderboard, earn points, and track their
collective impact. Contact us to discuss how to structure something that fits
your organization.

**What does an employer challenge look like?**
Participating employees download Shift, join your private group with an invite code,
and commute normally — the app handles all the tracking automatically. You receive
aggregate participation data: how many employees are active commuters, total active
trips, mode share across your team, and CO₂ avoided. Individual trip data is
always private.

**Can we run an ongoing program, not just a one-time event?**
Yes. Shift supports both time-limited challenges (like a month-long competition)
and standing employer programs where employees participate year-round. We'll work
with you on the structure that fits your goals.

**What information can we share with employees through the challenge?**
Employer groups can include curated content for members — local commuter benefits,
pre-tax transit pass information, nearby bike parking locations, and more.
We'll work with you to set this up as part of onboarding.

**Is there a cost?**
Employer challenges are offered on a fee-for-service basis. Contact us to discuss
what's right for your organization and team size.

**How is individual privacy protected?**
Employers receive only aggregate data. No individual employee's trips, routes,
Shift Rate, or points balance are ever visible to their employer. Participation
is voluntary — employees opt in by joining the group with the invite code.

**How does this connect to our ESG or sustainability reporting?**
Your employer dashboard provides verified active trip data by mode, total CO₂
avoided, and participation rates — figures you can use directly in sustainability
reports and employee wellness program documentation.

**We're interested. How do we get started?**
Contact us at [contact link] and we'll set up a conversation. We can typically
have an employer group configured within a week of agreeing on the parameters.

## Schools — /shift/schools page

**What is the Shift school program?**
The Shift school program turns "how did you get to school today?" into a
measurable, competitive, curriculum-aligned behavior change program. It runs
at the classroom, grade, and school level simultaneously — with weekly leaderboards,
monthly competitions, and end-of-competition celebrations.

**Do students need phones or accounts?**
No. The program is COPPA-clean by design. Students never touch a device for
logging purposes. Tracking happens on a physical wall chart with stickers or
magnets — one per student, one per day. No student accounts, no student devices,
no location data on any child.

**How much time does it take for teachers?**
Under 5 minutes per week. The teacher posts the tracking chart on Monday morning,
manages the daily sticker routine (students self-log in under 2 minutes as they
arrive), and photographs the completed chart on Friday afternoon. Shift handles
everything else automatically — data entry, leaderboard calculations, report
generation, and parent communications.

**What does the school get?**
Classroom, grade, and school leaderboards updated weekly. Auto-generated impact
reports for PTAs, newsletters, and school boards. Curriculum-aligned math and
science worksheets for K–2, 3–5, and 6–8 grade bands. A parent bridge — a weekly
email connecting families to their classroom's results. All materials are provided
by GSI at no cost.

**Is there a cost for schools?**
The Shift school program is free for participating schools. GSI provides all
materials, training, and ongoing support.

**How are parents involved?**
Parents receive a weekly email with their classroom's results and a link to view
the leaderboard. Parents can also join the Shift app to see their family's
contribution to the school totals and track their own active trips alongside
their kids' program.

**What grade levels does it cover?**
The program covers K–8 with curriculum-aligned worksheets for three grade bands:
K–2, 3–5, and 6–8. High school support is in development.

**What modes does the program track?**
Walk, bike, bus, and car. The chart uses simple icons — students mark how they
arrived each day. The program never shames car trips; it celebrates active ones.

**How does the leaderboard competition work?**
Classrooms compete within their grade, grades compete within the school, and
schools can compete against other participating schools. Competition runs in
monthly cycles. At the end of each cycle, Shift generates a results deck,
certificates for the winning classroom, and a school-level impact report.

**What is the benefit framing for students and families?**
The program leads with health, community, and independence — not environmental
messaging. Students learn about the physical benefits of active transportation
through the curriculum worksheets. Environmental impact is a secondary data point
available for older grades, not the primary hook.

**How do we get started?**
Contact us at [contact link] — we'll walk you through what's involved and get
your school set up. Onboarding a new school takes about 30 minutes of your time.
We handle everything else.

## Local businesses — (rewards catalog page or /shift page section)

**How does the rewards program work for businesses?**
Participating businesses offer a discount or perk to Shift users who redeem points
at their location. Users see your business in the Shift rewards catalog, come in,
and show their redemption confirmation on their phone. No POS integration required —
your staff just confirms the screen and honors the offer.

**What do businesses get out of participating?**
Visibility in the Shift rewards catalog to active commuters in your neighborhood.
Foot traffic from users specifically looking to redeem points. Brand association
with the GSI active commuting community. A monthly report showing redemption
counts and active user reach at your location.

**Is there a cost to participate?**
No. Participation in the Shift rewards network is free for local businesses.
You set the offer, we handle everything else.

**How do I know the trips are real?**
Shift validates trips automatically using location and motion data. Redemptions
are tied to verified active trip history — users can't inflate their points
through fake entries.
